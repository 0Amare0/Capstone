Initial Research:

Problem statement

Oakland leaders are unsure how their efforts to fight against homelessness have affected the homeless population.

Need statement

The City of Oakland needs to monitor the results of the solutions they have tried so they can create a strategy to effectively combat the homeless crisis.

Oakland's homeless population has increased over 24% in 3 years. 1,718 are sheltered and 3,337 are unsheltered. 240 fires have occured at the Wood Street homeless encampment, which happens to be close to Oxygen tanks owned by EBMUD.
Governor Newsom sent a letter to Oakland Leaders criticizing them for not handling the situation and "avoiding responsibility." City Auditor Courtney Ruby claims that a lack of data on how many people third party programs have helped makes it impossible to know what success looks like.
Oakland City Council President Nikki Fortunato Bas plans to gather the necesary information to effectively address the homeless crisis. Another council member, Dan Kalb believes that the issue lies in the lack of funding. The report issued by Courtney Ruby frames efficeient planning as the first step to combat homelessness in Oakland
